---
title: "Non-admin installation"
description: "How to get Clarity installed when you are not a workspace admin"
---

If you are not a Slack workspace admin, you need to request installation from someone who is.

## Why admin permission is needed

Slack requires workspace admin approval to install apps that access messages and channel data. This protects your workspace from unauthorized apps.

## How to request installation

<Steps>

<Step title="Find your workspace admin">
  In Slack, go to **Settings & administration** > **Manage members** to see who has admin privileges. Or ask in a general channel.
</Step>

<Step title="Share the install link">
  Send your admin the Clarity install link:

  ```
  https://clarityapp.io
  ```

  Explain that Clarity helps improve Slack communication with AI-powered coaching.
</Step>

<Step title="Wait for approval">
  Your admin reviews the app permissions and decides whether to install it. Once installed, Clarity is available to everyone in the workspace.
</Step>

<Step title="Set up your preferences">
  After installation, configure your own settings:

  1. Run `/clarity-settings`
  2. Select your coaching flags
  3. Enable auto coaching for your preferred channels
</Step>

</Steps>

## What to tell your admin

- **Purpose**: Clarity coaches users to communicate more effectively in Slack
- **Privacy**: Messages are analyzed in real time and not stored
- **Cost**: Free tier available. Pro is $4.99/month per workspace (not per user)
- **Permissions**: Requires access to messages in channels where coaching is enabled

## After installation

The person who installed Clarity becomes the workspace admin and can manage billing. Each user configures their own coaching preferences independently.

<Note>
Your settings are personal and do not affect teammates. Everyone in the workspace can choose their own flags and channels.
</Note>
